📄 How BIS Registration Works: Complete Guide

BIS Certification is mandatory for many products in India, especially those covered under Quality Control Orders (QCOs). It ensures that products meet safety and quality standards defined by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS).

Many businesses face shipment delays, penalties, or rejection due to missing BIS certification. Understanding the BIS registration process is essential for manufacturers and importers.

🔍 What is BIS Certification?

BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) is the national standards body of India established under the BIS Act, 2016. It ensures that products are safe, reliable, and meet defined Indian Standards.

⚖️ Mandatory Compliance (QCO)

The government enforces BIS certification through Quality Control Orders (QCOs). Once a product is listed, certification becomes compulsory.

❌ Selling non-certified products may result in:
• Stock seizure
• Penalties starting from ₹2 Lakhs
• Imprisonment up to 2 years

🏷️ Types of BIS Certification

1. ISI Mark (Scheme-I)
Applicable for products like steel, cement, chemicals, and toys. Requires factory inspection.

2. CRS Registration (Scheme-II)
Applicable for electronics like mobiles, laptops, and LED lights. Requires lab testing only.

⚙️ BIS Registration Process (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Identify IS Standard
Select the correct Indian Standard (IS Code) for your product.

Step 2: Product Testing
Send samples to BIS-recognized NABL labs for testing.

Step 3: Application Submission
Submit documents and reports on BIS portal.

Step 4: Factory Inspection
(Only for ISI Mark) BIS officer audits manufacturing facility.

Step 5: License Grant
BIS issues certification number (CM/L or R-number).

📑 Documents Required

• Business License / Factory Proof
• PAN & Aadhaar of authorized signatory
• Manufacturing process flowchart
• Machinery details
• Testing equipment list
• NABL lab test report
